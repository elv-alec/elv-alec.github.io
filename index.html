My Story

Hi there! I’m Alec, a fourth-year at UC Berkeley studying Electrical Engineering and Computer Science with a passion for technology, building cool things, and mixing music. At the moment, I’m a software engineer intern at Eluvio, a start-up that’s re-inventing how content is distributed across the internet; I’m currently working on web applications— specifically, I’m building an end-to-end OTP video streaming service and platform (you could say it’s like the Shopify for video streaming).

At UC Berkeley, I was fortunate to be involved in and take advantage of the plethora of opportunities to learn and grow. This past year, at Cal Hacks (the largest collegiate Hackathon), my team won first place using the Eluvio API with our product called aSend: a crowdsourced record compiler using Blockchain. Moreover, I was involved in the classroom by becoming an Undergraduate Academic Intern for the CS 61C (Machine Structures) course in which I was able to teach students in bi-weekly lab sessions. I would say a crucial part of my collegiate career was being involved with my fraternity, Zeta Psi, where I was able to develop a large network and grow as a leader by holding positions on the executive board.

In my free time, I enjoy building side projects with my friends, reading, exploring nature, playing poker, and honing my skills as a DJ. For my personal projects, I prefer building practical apps that I can use in my everyday life; for instance, I created an app where I can track the routes of my outdoor runs and hikes that I can then share with my friends. Presently, I’m building an iMessage App that makes splitting purchases with friends easier through Venmo.

Feel free to check out my recent work or connect with me below.
