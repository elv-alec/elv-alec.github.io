My Story

Hi there! I’m Alec, a fourth-year at UC Berkeley studying Electrical Engineering and Computer Science with a passion for technology, building cool things, and mixing music. At the moment, I’m a software engineer at Eluvio, working on web development. I'm also collaborating with Interscope Records to develop an iOS app to promote an artist's upcoming album.

At UC Berkeley, I was involved in the classroom by becoming an Undergraduate Academic Intern for the CS 61C (Machine Structures) course in which I was able to teach students in bi-weekly lab sessions. This past year, at Cal Hacks (the largest collegiate Hackathon), my team won first place using the Eluvio API with our product called aSend: a crowdsourced record compiler using Blockchain. Moreover, I would say a crucial part of my collegiate career was being involved with my fraternity, Zeta Psi, where I was able to develop a large network and grow as a leader by holding positions on the executive board.

In my free time, I enjoy building side projects with my friends, reading, exploring nature, and honing my skills as a DJ. Presently, I’m building an iMessage App that makes splitting purchases with friends easier through Venmo.

Feel free to check out my recent work or connect with me below.

Cell Tower Network

  • Python Script that decides which cities to build cell towers in and lay out the fiber network between cities based on restrictions.
  • The problem states that there must be a cell tower in every major city or a neighboring city, this network must be a tree, and you want to minimize the average pairwise distance between these cell towers.
  • I came up with a Minimum Routing Cost Spanning Tree Algorithm coupled with Prim's and Kruskal's Algorithm with pruning that generated this network.
